The Tollywood Actor

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (born 20 May 1983), also known as Jr NTR or Tarak, is an Indian actor, singer, and television presenter who works in Telugu Cinema. In his film career spanning 20 years, Rama Rao has worked in over 29 films. He is mostly known for his single take acting, dialogue delivery and dancing without rehearsals. He is popularly referred as Young Tiger of Tollywood.[1] Ntr has received two state Nandi Awards, two Filmfare Awards South and four CineMAA Awards. One of the highest paid Telugu film actors,[2][3] he was ranked 28th in Forbes India Celebrity 100 list with an annual income of ₹280 million in 2018.[4] Grandson of Telugu actor-politician N. T. Rama Rao,[5] Rama Rao Jr. played the lead role as a child actor in Ramayanam (1996) which won the National Film Award for Best Children's Film for that year. His debut as an adult with Ninnu Choodalani (2001) and tasted success with the film Student No. 1 (2001). He received critical reception for his performance in works such as Aadi (2002), Simhadri (2003), Rakhi (2006), Yamadonga (2007), Adhurs (2010), Brindavanam (2010), Temper (2015), Nannaku Prematho (2016), Janatha Garage (2016), Jai Lava Kusa (2017) and Aravinda Sametha Veera Raghava (2018).

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao, byname NTR

Nimmakuru, near Machilipatnam, India—died January 18, 1996, Hyderabad), Indian motion-picture actor and director, politician, and government official who founded the Telugu Desam Party (TDP) and served three terms (1983–84; 1984–89; and 1994–95) as chief minister (head of government) of Andhra Pradesh state in southeastern India. As an actor he rose to superstardom among the country’s Telugu-speaking people and parlayed his fame into a successful political career. The man who would became popularly known as NTR was born in a small village near the coastal city of Machilipatnam (now in Andhra Pradesh) to a poor farming family. He completed a bachelor’s degree from Andhra Christian College in nearby Guntur. After qualifying for the civil service in what was then the Madras Presidency under British India, he took a job as a sub-registrar at Mangalagiri, northeast of Guntur. He soon left that post, however, to pursue a career in acting. NTR’s first film, Mana Desam, was released in 1949, and he continued to make movies until 1982, appearing in some 300 films and directing more than a dozen. He quickly demonstrated enormous popular appeal and soon attained cult-figure status. He often portrayed a Hindu god, particularly Krishna, in his movies, and his adoption of the white or saffron robes of his costumes as his everyday dress only reinforced his lofty image. In March 1982 he founded the TDP, with the stated objective of protecting the interests of India’s Telugu-speaking people. The TDP turned out to be highly successful at consolidating voters against the then-ruling Indian National Congress (Congress Party) in the state. Riding on NTR’s popularity, TDP-affiliated candidates (contesting as independents, since the party was still unregistered) won a large majority of seats in the 1983 elections to the Andhra Pradesh legislative assembly. The party subsequently formed a government, with NTR as the first non-Congress chief minister since the establishment of the state in 1956.

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao, byname NTR

Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao Jr., popularly known as Jr. NTR, is an Indian film actor, Kuchipudi dancer, playback singer and television personality known for his works in Telugu cinema. He is the grandson of Telugu actor, and former chief minister of Andhra Pradesh. NTR starred his acting career as a child artist in Ramayanam (1996), and won the National Film Award for Best Children's Film for that year. He debuted as an adult in 2000 with the film Ninnu Choodalani. He has has worked in around twenty six films and has won one state award, two Filmfare Best Telugu Actor Award and four CineMAA Awards so far. He his also a member of his grandfather's political party, the Telugu Desam Party. Jr. NTR made his Television debut as the host of the Bigg Boss Telugu that is being broadcast on Star Maa channel. The show began airing on Star MAA from July 16, 2017.

Balakrishna Nandamuri

Nandamuri Balakrishna, also known as NBK or simply Balakrishna, is an Indian film actor and politician known for his works mainly in the Telugu cinema. He is the sixth son of Telugu film actor and former Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh N. T. Rama Rao. He entered the film industry as a child artist at the age of fourteen with the film Tatamma Kala (1974). Nandamuri Balakrishna is known for his dialogue delivery skills and has acted in more than hundred feature films in a variety of roles. Ever since the Telugu Desam Party formation, Balakrishna always campaigned for it in every election, but never entered the electoral battle.
